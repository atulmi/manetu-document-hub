# 2026 Compensation Bands

**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — HR, executive team, and hiring managers only
**Prepared by:** Lisa Tran, VP People
**Effective date:** January 1, 2026 (pending board approval)

## Overview

These bands reflect a 12% increase over 2025 levels to remain competitive in the current market. AI/ML specialist roles receive an additional 8% premium based on market data showing significant demand-driven inflation in this segment.

## Engineering Bands

### Software Engineering

| Level | Title | Base Salary | Equity (4yr) | Annual Bonus |
|-------|-------|------------|--------------|-------------|
| L3 | Software Engineer | $130K–$155K | $40K–$60K | 10% |
| L4 | Senior Engineer | $160K–$190K | $80K–$120K | 12% |
| L5 | Staff Engineer | $195K–$230K | $150K–$200K | 15% |
| L6 | Principal Engineer | $235K–$275K | $250K–$350K | 18% |

## Bonus Structure

- Annual bonuses are paid in Q1 based on prior-year performance
- Bonus targets are percentages of base salary
- Individual multiplier: 0.5x–1.5x based on performance rating
- Company multiplier: 0.8x–1.2x based on ARR achievement vs. target

## Equity Details

- All equity is in the form of ISO stock options
- Four-year vesting with one-year cliff
- Exercise price set at current 409A valuation ($8.50/share as of last appraisal)
- Refresh grants available annually at manager discretion (typically 25% of initial grant)

## Key Changes from 2025

1. Overall bands increased 12% to match market movement
2. New AI/ML premium band created (8% above standard engineering)
3. L6/Principal level added to engineering IC track (previously topped at L5)
4. Bonus target for L5+ increased from 12% to 15%

## Budget Impact

- Total incremental cost of band adjustments: ~$1.2M annually
- Funded by revenue growth — no impact on profitability targets
- Board approval required before communicating new bands to employees
