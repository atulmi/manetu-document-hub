# M&A Target Analysis: TechCo Inc

**Classification:** CONFIDENTIAL — Executive team and board only
**Prepared by:** James Park, VP Product
**Date:** December 2025

## Target Overview

**Company:** TechCo Inc
**Founded:** 2021
**Headquarters:** Austin, TX
**Employees:** 28 (18 engineering)
**Annual Revenue:** ~$2.8M ARR
**Funding:** Seed ($3M from Y Combinator + angels)

TechCo builds a multi-tenant policy management platform that allows SaaS companies to define and enforce per-customer access rules. Their product handles tenant isolation, role hierarchy inheritance, and dynamic policy composition — capabilities that align closely with our 2026 multi-tenant roadmap.

## Strategic Rationale

### Why TechCo?

1. **Accelerate multi-tenant policies** — our roadmap estimates 8 weeks to build multi-tenant support from scratch. TechCo's existing platform could cut this to 3 weeks of integration work, saving 5 weeks of engineering time.

2. **Talent acquisition** — TechCo's engineering team includes 4 engineers with deep expertise in policy evaluation systems. Hiring these individuals on the open market would take 6+ months and cost $150K+ in recruiting fees.

3. **Customer base** — TechCo has 12 paying customers, including 3 in the fintech vertical where we want to expand. These could convert to DataVault customers with a migration path.

4. **IP and patents** — TechCo holds two pending patents on dynamic policy composition and hierarchical role resolution that would strengthen our competitive moat.

## Valuation Analysis

| Method | Estimated Value |
|--------|----------------|
| Revenue multiple (10x ARR) | $28M |
| Comparable transactions | $22M–$32M |
| DCF (5-year projection) | $25M |
| **Recommended offer range** | **$24M–$28M** |

TechCo's investors paid $3M at seed. A $24M–$28M acquisition represents an 8–9x return, which should be attractive to their cap table.

## Funding the Acquisition

- **Option A:** Use existing cash ($12.4M) plus Series B proceeds. This requires accelerating the fundraise.
- **Option B:** Stock-heavy deal (60% Acme equity, 40% cash). Reduces cash outlay but dilutes existing shareholders.
- **Option C:** Defer acquisition to Q3 2026 after Series B closes and 2026 revenue targets are de-risked.

**Recommendation:** Option C — defer until we have Series B capital. This avoids depleting our cash reserves and gives us stronger negotiating leverage.

## Integration Risks

1. **Cultural alignment** — TechCo is a small, fast-moving startup. Integration into our larger engineering org could cause attrition.
2. **Technical debt** — preliminary code review suggests TechCo's codebase uses an older Node.js version and lacks comprehensive test coverage.
3. **Customer migration** — TechCo's existing customers may resist migrating to DataVault if it disrupts their workflows.
4. **Timeline** — full integration is estimated at 4–6 months, during which engineering bandwidth for new feature work would be reduced.
