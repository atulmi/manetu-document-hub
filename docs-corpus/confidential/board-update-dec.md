# Board Update — December 2025

## OKR Progress

### Company-Level OKRs

**O1: Achieve $15M ARR by end of 2025**
- Current ARR: $15.8M — **exceeded target by 5%**
- Key driver: Enterprise tier growth and strong net revenue retention (118%)

**O2: Launch DataVault 3.0 with AI agent capabilities**
- Status: **On track** — GA release scheduled for December 18
- Beta feedback from 15 customers has been overwhelmingly positive
- Three customers have committed to upgrading to Professional tier for AI features

**O3: Reach cash-flow positive operations**
- Status: **Achieved in Q3** — first positive operating quarter ($1.1M net income)
- Sustainable at current trajectory without additional funding

**O4: Grow engineering team to 50 by year-end**
- Current headcount: 42 engineers (84% of target)
- Shortfall due to intentional hiring pause to protect profitability in Q3
- Hiring pipeline is strong; expect to reach 55 by end of Q1 2026

## Fundraising Status

Given our cash-flow positive position, fundraising is optional but strategic:

- **Current cash:** $12.4M
- **Series B conversations:** Preliminary discussions with three firms (Benchmark, Sequoia, Accel)
- **Indicative terms:** $30M–$40M at $180M–$220M pre-money valuation
- **Recommendation:** Delay formal process to Q2 2026 to negotiate from a stronger ARR position ($18M+ projected)

The board should discuss whether to pursue a preemptive round now at favorable terms or wait for better leverage. My recommendation is to wait — we do not need the capital, and six more months of growth data significantly strengthens our position.

## Competitive Landscape

- **Datadog** announced "AI Document Insights" in their fall release — basic document summarization, no policy engine
- **Splunk** acquired a small MCP tooling company — likely 12–18 months from a competitive product
- **Our moat:** integrated policy engine (MPE) is 2+ years ahead of any competitor's access control for AI agents
